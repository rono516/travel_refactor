    KENYA INTRODUCTION

    Kenya is an enchanting country and few places in the world can better and march its varied touristic attraction.


    The overwhelming of the wildlife sanctuaries, glorious beaches, breathtaking scenery in a vast and unspoilt land, an almost perfect climate, sophisticated accommodation and a hospitable happy people.


    Kenya contains almost every known land form from true glacial ice to arid desert, mountain massifs to rich savannahs, large lakes and dense forests.


    The Great Rift Valley, its floors littered with both lovely fresh and salty lakes, dormant volcanoes, traverses this great country from north to south. Mt Kenya (5,199 metres/ 17,058 feet) with its snow capped Batian and Nelion peaks stands sentinel above rolling grassland plains and the fascinating semi-arid frontiers of the north. Lake Victoria (worlds 2nd largest lake after Lake Superior of USA and Africa's largest lake) and Lake Turkana now known as the cradle of man all lie in this great valley, the Indian Ocean coast, a place of magnificent colourful beaches turquoise seas and archaeological antiquities.

    Above all are the wildlife sanctuaries, the National parks devoted to the absolute protection of the indigenous flora and fauna and the National Reserves, where wildlife co-exists in perfect harmony with the pastoral nomadic local communities since time immemorial. All these wildlife protected areas cover 7% of Kenya's landmass. They vary in abundance and variety of their wildlife, scenery, climate and attitude; from the famous Masai Mara now the 7th New World Wonder of our modern times to the ornithological paradise of Lake Nakuru and Samburu plus Shaba in the arid north.


    Variation is altitude and terrain great climatic contrasts from the hot and humid coast to the fresh and sometimes frosty mornings of the Central Highlands and the dry baking of the northern deserts .In overall it is seldom harsh, neither too hot nor with long sun filled days – an almost perfect climate. The country is very agricultural in its nature, practicing both commercial and subsistence farming. Cash crops include: coffee, tea, sugar cane, pyrethrum, cotton, tobacco and of course flowers.


    Subsistence crops are mostly for local consumption i.e maize, millet, sorghum, groundnuts etc.
